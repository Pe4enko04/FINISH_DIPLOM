Literature

The writer's creative biography began on April 6, 1952, with the story "Newsman Juido" published in the newspaper "Komsomolets of Kyrgyzstan". Aitmatov wrote his first literary text in Russian, one of his two native languages. After graduating from the institute in 1953, Chingiz Aitmatov, a senior livestock specialist at the Kyrgyz Research Institute of Animal Husbandry, continued to write stories in Russian and Kyrgyz, publishing texts in local publications. In 1956, he decided to improve the qualifications of the writer and went to Moscow, where he entered the Higher Literary Courses. In parallel with his studies, he wrote a lot. Already in June 1957, the magazine "Ala-Too" published the first story of the young writer "Face to Face". In the same year, Jamila was published - it is interesting that the story that made the writer famous was first published in French translation. The writer graduated from literary courses in 1958. By the time she received her diploma, two novels and short stories had been published in Russian. Aitmatov's first novel will be released only in 1980. In the novel "And the Day Lasts Longer than a Century" realistic events of the life of Buranny Edigey are intertwined with a fantastic line of contact between humanity and an alien civilization. One gets the impression that it is easier for people to reach an understanding with aliens than to agree among themselves. Secondarily, the writer returned to the genre of science fiction in the mid-nineties, writing "Cassandra's Brand" - a story about the creation of artificial people. The rest of the works are written in the genre of realism. In the Soviet Union, realism was socialist, but Aitmatov is too pessimistic for socialist realism. His heroes live and suffer for real, without turning into vigorous builders of communism. The protagonist of The White Steamship, a boy who believes in fairy tales, dies when his Deer is killed. In general, folk tales and legends are an important part of Aitmatov's plots. Mythological images are sometimes brighter than the main characters. From the legend of the harsh invaders who turned captives into slaves, deprived of independence and memory, the word and concept of "mankurt" migrated into the Russian language - a person who has forgotten his roots. Aitmatov's second novel, Scaffold, was published in 1986. During this period in the USSR, Mikhail Gorbachev began Perestroika, and it became possible to write about the problems of the country. But even against the background of permitted publicity, The Scaffold produces a striking effect - the novel raises several acute issues at once, talks about drug addiction and corruption, about faith and ministers of the church.
