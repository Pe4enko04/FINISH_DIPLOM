Childhood and youth

Chingiz Torekulovich Aitmatov was born on December 12, 1928 in the family of communists Torekul Aitmatov and Nagima Khamzievna Aitmatova (nee Abduvalieva) in the village of Sheker, Kara-Buurinsky (Kirovsky) district, Talas canton of the Kirghiz ASSR. After the birth of Chingiz, the family moved to the city, as his father went on a promotion: since 1929, Torekul Aitmatov's career has been rapidly going up.

In 1933, he was already the second secretary of the Kirghiz regional committee of the CPSU (b). In 1935, the young leader became a student at the Institute of Red Professors in Moscow, the family also moved to the capital of the USSR. During this time, Nagima gave birth to her husband's son Ilgiz, twins Reva and Lucia (the boy died in infancy) and daughter Rosa. In 1937, at the insistence of her husband, Nagima Khamzievna moved the children to relatives in Sheker.

The father of the future writer was arrested in September 1937 on suspicion of anti-Soviet nationalist activities and transferred to Frunze (the capital of Soviet Kyrgyzstan). On November 5, 1938, they were shot. The wife of the "enemy of the people" was struck in her rights, but all the children of the repressed political worker received a higher education and each wrote their own page in history.

During the Second World War, all adult men were mobilized, and the fourteen-year-old Chingiz turned out to be one of the most literate people in the village and took the post of secretary of the village council. After the war, the young man was able to continue his studies: after a rural eight-year-old, he graduated with honors from the Dzhambul zootechnical school and in 1948 entered the Kyrgyz Agricultural Institute in Frunze.
